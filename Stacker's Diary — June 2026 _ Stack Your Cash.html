About This Column

This is my real portfolio. Real decisions. Real mistakes. And — where they happen — real wins.

Stacker’s Diary is where I write honestly about the investment decisions I’m actually making, the thinking behind them, and what I’m learning along the way. It’s not a model portfolio or a set of recommendations — it’s a journal. My journal, shared with members so that the process of investing is demystified a little more with each entry.

You’ll find entries on why I bought or sold something, how I think about risk and allocation, moments where I got it wrong and what I took from it, and the broader thinking that shapes how I invest. No performance theatre. No hindsight polishing. Just the honest version.

Entries are published roughly monthly, typically at the end of each month or when something significant happens worth writing about.

This Month’s Commentary

What the Portfolio Did This Month

June was the first full month of tracking Stacker’s Diary properly. The portfolio is heavily positioned in investment trusts across a range of sectors — technology (PCT), global equity income (FCIT, MYI), UK equity income (CTY), infrastructure (INPP, CORD), and specialist areas including mining (BRWM), Asia-Pacific (FCSS) and frontiers markets (BRFI).

The active fund holdings — Fidelity Global Dividend and L&G Asia Pacific Equity Income — complement the trust exposure with more liquid, daily-priced positions. The gilt allocation (T30, T35, T33H) provides some duration and income ballast alongside the elevated cash position.

It’s worth being upfront about something that shapes the whole character of this portfolio: this is part of my pension pot, and I am a couple of years away from retirement. That context matters. The decisions I’m making aren’t purely about maximising long-term growth — they’re increasingly about managing risk, protecting what I’ve built, and gradually transitioning to a portfolio that can support income in retirement. You’ll see that philosophy running through everything I do here.

Decisions Made

The derisking process is already underway. Last month I sold three positions as part of a deliberate effort to reduce exposure and simplify the portfolio as I approach retirement:

Lazard Global Listed Infrastructure Eq A Dist — exited at a small loss. The infrastructure theme remains in the portfolio through INPP and CORD, but I wanted to consolidate into trusts I know better and reduce the overall number of positions.

UK 5.25% Gilt 31/01/41 (T41F) — sold at a small loss. This was a longer-dated gilt and the duration risk felt excessive given where I am in the investment cycle. I retain shorter-dated gilt exposure through T30, T35 and T33H.

JPMorgan European Growth & Income (JEGI) — exited at a small loss. European equity exposure remains a consideration but this position had been underperforming and I chose to take the exit.

Earlier this month I also took the buyback option on BlackRock Latin American Ord (BRLA), exiting at a 26% profit having held for approximately a year. That was a satisfying outcome from a high-conviction, higher-risk position that did what I hoped it would. The proceeds have added to the cash buffer.

The elevated cash position (23.47%) reflects both these recent exits and a deliberate decision not to redeploy immediately. The Personal Assets Trust (PNL) — which holds gold, short-dated gilts and quality equities — also reflects the more defensive posture I’m moving towards.

One area where I have been actively trading is BlackRock World Mining Trust (BRWM). I’ve been adding and selling around a core position over recent months, benefiting from the rising gold price in what have been uncertain and volatile markets. Gold has performed strongly as a safe haven, and BRWM gives exposure to that alongside broader commodities. I run this alongside PNL, which also carries meaningful gold exposure as part of its defensive mandate. Between the two, I maintain a deliberate and steady allocation to gold and real assets — not as speculation, but as a genuine hedge against the kind of uncertainty that tends to hurt everything else at the same time.

What I’m Watching

Three things on my radar heading into July:

1. Infrastructure trust discounts — INPP and CORD are both trading at discounts to NAV. If the rate environment stabilises, these could rerate meaningfully. I’m monitoring both for a potential top-up, but with the retirement clock ticking I’m weighing whether to add or use any further recovery as an exit opportunity.

2. UK gilt yields — the three gilt positions are short-to-medium dated. Any BoE rate cut would benefit the existing holdings. As I derisk further I may look to extend the gilt allocation modestly as a source of predictable income.

3. China exposure — FCSS has been a drag. I’m reassessing the thesis and will decide in July whether to hold, reduce, or exit. Given the move towards a lower-risk portfolio ahead of retirement, the case for keeping a specialist single-country emerging market position is getting harder to justify.

I’ve been investing long enough to know that doing nothing is sometimes the most active decision you can make. The cash isn’t idle — it’s waiting for the right moment.

— Chris, Stacker’s Diary · June 2026
